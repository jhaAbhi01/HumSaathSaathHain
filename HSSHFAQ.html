Frequently Asked Questions (FAQs)

  • Is depression real?

    • - Yes, it is very real and a serious issue

  • Is depression an illness? 

    • - Yes, it falls under the category of “mentally ill”.

  • Is depression curable? 

    • - Yes it is.

  • Can depression be cured without the help of doctors? 

    • - It is preferred to consult a psychiatrist, however, it can be cured otherwise too.

  • Is Yoga helpful?

    •  - Yes. Yoga and other medications do help to divert thoughts

  • Does medication cause side effects? 

    • - Depending on the amount of required dosage. There are a few medicines which don’t cause side effects, but in most cases, there might be minimal side effects.

  • How do I differentiate whether I am sad or depressed? 

    • - Depression is a mental state, while sadness is an emotion. Sadness has an antonym, happiness. Depression doesn’t have an antonym. Prolonged sadness can be a symptom of depression.

  • Can kids/senior citizens/physically disabled/LGBTQ community have depression? 

    • - Yes. All of them can suffer through depression. Kids hardly have a chance though, generally kids below 12 might not go through it.

  • Is depression contagious? 

    • - No, not all. However, discussing depressing topics with a patient might make you a little depressed. 

  • Is depression hereditary? 

    • - No.

  • Am I depressed because I am just too busy? 

    • - Being busy is a possible factor as it makes you tired. However, being busy is one of the suggested remedies for a depressed person as it helps you divert your thoughts.

  • How does depression affect me physically? 

    • - A depressed person often tends to mess up his sleep schedule and diet. This leads to physical issues.

  • How do I tell my friends/parents that I am depressed? 

    • - Open up. Be free. If you feel your parents won’t take it seriously, or wouldn’t believe you, then approach them through a counsellor. Friends are the best to open up as they are of the same age and won’t judge you.

  • Would consuming sleeping pills make matters worse?

    • - There is no way they would make matters better at least. Sleeping pills is just a temporary solution over insomnia and does have side effects if used frequently.

  • Do psychiatrists inform my parents regarding whatever I say? 

    • - Not without your consent. Confidentiality is a priority hence you can tell a counsellor everything. Counsellor will discuss it with parents only if you tell them that you are okay with it.

  • Does a trip help? 

    • - Yes. Refreshments do help improve your mental state.

  • How do I keep myself away from addiction?

    • - In a state like that, a patient might tend to resort to a certain addiction thinking that it would be his way out through it. But diversions should preferably be positive than addictions, which would pull you from one hole and put you into another. Tell yourselves that addiction is not a solution, and consult a psychiatrist.

  • Is my love life a reason behind my state?

    • - There is a very high probability of this being the case, as love life is the weakest link in the lives of most of the people. You’ll learn to deal with it.

  • Is there any discussion group where depressed people can discuss their issues? 

    • - Yes, a few websites do provide group chat features.
